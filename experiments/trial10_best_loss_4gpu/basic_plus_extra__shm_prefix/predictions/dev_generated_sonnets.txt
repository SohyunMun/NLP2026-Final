Thine eyes I love, and they, as pitying me,
Knowing thy heart torment me with disdain,
Have put on black, and loving mourners be,
Are trembling from their own wrath. And so loved are those vain souls of mind!--

1
Beshrew that heart that makes my heart to groan
For that deep wound it gives my friend and me.
Is 't not enough to torture me alone, a prisoner?

2
So, now I have confessed that he is thine
And I myself am mortgaged to thy will,
Myself I'll forfeit, so that other mine in this estimation? – my life may be undone.

3
Whoever hath her wish, thou hast thy will,
And will to boot, and will in overplus.
More than enough am I that vex thee still,

4
If thy soul check thee that I come so near,
Swear to thy blind soul that I was thy will,
And will, thy soul knows, is admitted there.

5
Thou blind fool, Love, what dost thou to mine eyes
That they behold and see not what they see?
They know what beauty is, see where it lies, but never leave;
See how the moon marks her light in all places:
And yet when we feel that our spirits touch these false truths —
I will straightway at thee pale sun from my mind.

6
When my love swears that she is made of truth
I do believe her though I know she lies,
That she might think me some untutored youth, While you are not mine.

My heart bleeds for this woman—the worthiest summer! Which was less than a thousand years ago; It died as young and old if in time it had been born before man came to us — When men stood up yet again… But now: the lives with whom we have all lived which give pause lost hope when their whole timidity shone on them.— The Lady whose beauty means can scarce be spilt out by your sorrow's light ♦

7
O, call not me to justify the wrong
That thy unkindness lays upon my heart;
Wound me not with thine eye but with thy tongue;
Thy lovestrife even more worthy than mine wicked fingers.

8
Be wise as thou art cruel; do not press
My tongue-tied patience with too much disdain,
Lest sorrow lend me words, and words express my faults: pity the young moon's name.

9
In faith, I do not love thee with mine eyes,
For they in thee a thousand errors note;
But 'tis my heart that loves what they despise, and who is the niest:
My happy words to God are of your blood.

10
Love is my sin, and thy dear virtue hate,
Hate of my sin, grounded on sinful loving.
O, but with mine compare thou thine own state, if this life be'd well: for what it's doth stain in the grave; I will kill myself by a man like thee! Let us die as our lives should upon dead bodies or ashes—though so long we live to please eternal souls… But let me have peace before that day shall come when all men cease gracefully from him!'
