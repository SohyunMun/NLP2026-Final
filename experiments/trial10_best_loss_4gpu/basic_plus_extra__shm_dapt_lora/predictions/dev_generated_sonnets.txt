Beshrew that heart that makes my heart to groan
For that deep wound it gives my friend and me.
Is 't not enough to torture me alone, as I desire?
Thus angry shall be the world when all our trials are longed for;
And yet remain locked in those pitiable loving struggles: how then can we say Heaven is finally finished!

Be wise as thou art cruel; do not press
My tongue-tied patience with too much disdain,
Lest sorrow lend me words, and words express—
And if you come to hand out laws or moral principles
That I can take away from thee in vain, let it be a truth.
